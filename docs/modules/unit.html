The Unit type exists for the cases where you want a type-safe equivalent of undefined or null. It's a concrete instance, which won't blow up on you, and you can safely use it with e.g. Result without being concerned that you'll accidentally introduce null or undefined back into your application.

Equivalent to () or "unit" in many functional or functional-influenced languages.
